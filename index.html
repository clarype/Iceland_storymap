Katie and I are traveling to Iceland!

Day 2 : Reykjavik

6.21.2023

We hit the ground running in Reyjavik. It was a day that seemed a lot longer than normal due to a lack of sleep and a sun that would not set.

Day 3 : Reykjavik 2

This day was far lower key and it was welcomed. It was fill with more geothermal pool time, conventional culture exposure, and window shopping.

Day 4 : To Thingvellir and Back to Reykjavik

A jam packed day on snorkeling between the North American and the Eurasian continent, geothermal pool time, geisirs, and watersfalls.

Day 5 : Starting the Ring Road

The first leg of the ring road took us to more geothermal springs, a volcanic crater, and more water falls. Iceland is truly a young landscape.

Day 6 :

Headed to the Westman Islands to find Puffins and checkout the island the had a volcanic eruption that cover the town in 5 feet of ash and lava flow that destroyed part of the town.

Day 7 :

From Vik we explored ice caves, saw man made lava, and saw a glaical landscape that brought Katie to tears.

Day 8 :

One our was to Hofn we encountered heavy winds that forced us to stay in the van most of the day for a much needed rest. But that did not stop us from finding a awesome restaurant and swimming at the local pool.

Day 9 :

Left Hofn after a major wind/rain storm and head to the Eastfjords where we stoppped at Petra's Stone Collection. Then headed north to Seydisfjordur a cute fishing harbour turned artsy town.

Day 10 :

Onward from Seydisfjordur to see more waterfalls, soak in hot pools and traveling off into rural country to find ponies to ride.

Day 11 :

There was a lot of ground covered today on our way to Akureyri. We start by riding ponies then we went to a geothermal power plant and saw more hot spring. A fun day and katie's horse was emo but a sweet heart!

Day 12 :

We started the day by strolling around Akureyri's botanical garden and downtown where I found some art! Next we head north to a viking hot bath and then head west to Lambeyri at campground south of Varmahlíð.
